About

     is a 3D online fighting game that supports up to 4 players in a free-for-all. You play as a wizard with a selection of 4 chosen spells, and you fight other wizards in a battle arena until only one wizard remains.

     was also entered in a competition, the IEEE GameSIG 2017 Showcase, and ended as a semi-finalist.

Usage

Creating and Joining Games

    To play , you either create a lobby for other players to join, or look for one yourself. You can do this from the main menu. When creating a lobby, you will see a new menu. Here, you can see the players currently in the lobby, and your spells. You can choose your spell loadout from here before the game starts. Joining an existing lobby will lead you to the same menu, but you will not be able to start the game yourself.

The main menu. You can create a lobby, join another lobby, customize your spells, and change your settings from here.

The lobby with two players.

Gameplay

     is primarily a fighting game; thus, players fight each other until there is only one left. To do that, you must move your character around to dodge spells and reposition, and cast your own spells. To move your player, you right-click at a point on the ground you wish to move to. The character will automatically turn and move towards that point.

    To cast a spell, you press 1, 2, 3, or 4 to prime that spell, and then left-click at a point on the ground, or at another character. The spell will then be cast, and got on cooldown. Most spells require a target of some kind, but some are cast on yourself automatically.

    You are killed if you lose all of your health by taking damage from enemy spells. Once killed, you must wait a few seconds before respawning. If you lose all of your lives, you have lost the game.

A player casts Fireball at another player.

Spells

    There are a variety of spells in the game, each grouped by element. The spells:

  • Fireball: Hurl a fiery projectile.
  • Meteor: Call down a flaming meteor.
  • Alchemist's Blood: Your spells burn their targets.
  • Plasma Orb: Emit a slow moving ball.
  • Voidwalk: You move faster and become invulnerable, but cannot cast spells.
  • Entropic Decay: Increase damage taken by enemies and slow them.
  • Quark: Fire a small, fast projectile.
  • Innervate: You move much faster.
  • Lightning Strike: Call down a bolt of lightning.

A player casts Quark.

Spell Selection

    You can set your spell loadouts by clicking on the Spells button in the main menu. Here, you can drag and drop spells into each of your loadouts. You can also see what each spell does, their damage, and their cooldown times.

Customizing the spell loadout. The player has Voidwalk selected.

Development

Overview

     was made in C# with Unity3D. We used several outside assets, including Photon, Magic Missiles, and 3 Skyboxes. The player models, the arena, and the main menu were all created by Christian Sydow, and the music was made by Cyrus Fiete.

Player Character

    The player character is made up of several scripts. There is a single controller that interacts with botht the UI and the character data. This controller allows the player to move around, cast spells, and take damage. The controller reads and updates the character data. This data contains all of the character's stats, like movement speed, health, and damage bonuses. The controller updates the character UI, by updating the health, and setting the cooldowns of his/her spells.

The character UI. Here you can see the health on the left, and the four spells.

    The player movement and casting is similar to a MOBA-like system, where the players move around by right-clicking, and cast spells by priming that spell and then left-clicking.

Spell System

    The spells are created by matching several different components together. Each spell needs the effect, and a delivery method. This delivery method can be in the form of a projectile, or by a status. Projectile spells transfer their effect logic onto the projectile, in the form of an on-hit method. Status spells directly affect their targets. Each spell has a data structure that contains that spells stats, and a controller which determines how the spell works. This controller will either directly execute its effect onto their target, or spawn a projectile and off-load the effect onto it.

    For example, the Fireball spell spawns a flaming projectile, which then damages the first enemy it hits. The Meteor spell spawns a flaming meteor high above the arena, which then falls towards the ground and explodes upon impact. The Innervate spell does not spawn any projectile. Instead, it tells the caster's controller to increase the character's movement speed.

    Many spells give temporary changes to a character's stats. This is handled by the character's controller. When a temporary effect of some kind targets a character, that effect is stored in the controller. At each tick, the controller checks the effects stored to see if any have expired. Upon expiration, the effect is reversed. Each effect has an effect and inverse-effect method for this purpose.

    Different spells also have different targeting methods. For example, the Fireball spell requires a target direction, but does not care about individual points. On the other hand, the Meteor spell requires a target point within range. The Innervate spell does not require a target, as the target is always the caster character.

Networking

    Networking was done by Photon. When players create or join a lobby, they do this via methods provided by Photon. In-game, whenever a player performs an action, such as move, or casting a spell, the player's client informs Photon, which then informs all of the other clients. This also occurs when a player takes damage, when a projectile moves or dissipates, and when a player dies.

Future

     is completed for now. There were many planned features prior to the creation of this game that are not present in this release. Among them were:

  • Spell creation: Instead of spells, you have spell components. You mix and match components to create custom spells.
  • Collectible spell components: At the end of each game, each player gets a random component. You can collect these components and create spells out of them. The rarer the component, the more unique and powerful their effects were.
  • More spell elements: There are only three elements present in the release: fire, gravity, and lightning. There were more elements planned, like cold. Each element would have a theme and specialty, and every spell component would inherit that specialty.
  • More spell effects: Currently, the effects of the spells are simplistic. They are either deal damage as a projectile, deal damage in an area, and buff or debuff a target. There were plans for summoning stationary objects that performed the effect, as opposed to a projectile, forcibly pushing and pulling enemies, and stuns and other crowd control effects.
