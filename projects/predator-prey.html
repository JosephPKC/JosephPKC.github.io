About

     is a GUI simulation of a fictional predator-prey environment. This is loosely related to the Lotka-Volterra equations, or the predator-prey equations. The world comprises of several creatures, each with their own methods of eating and breeding. This was made for a course in intermediate C++ at PCC (CS 3A)

Usage

    Using is easy: just press start. The world will be initialized randomly, with set initial population numbers. Once set, every creature just goes about their lives: moving, eating, breeding, and dying. This simply continues until you stop or exit.

    There are many creatures that inhabit the world of :

  • Prey: Generic creatures that do not hunt other creatures. They are instead the basis of all life in this world, just eating grass from the ground when hungry.
  • Predator: Generic predator that hunts prey creatures. Whenever they are hungry, they kill a nearby prey creature.
  • Hunter: An apex predator. They hunt all creatures, even their own kind. Just like predators, they kill a nearby creature when hungry.
  • Infected: A special creature that has its own rules. They do not hunger nor breed. Instead, they infect other creatures and assimilate them into their own kind.

Development

Overview

     was made in C++ via Qt Creator. It ensures that every creature gets to do something in the world, and updates the GUI accordingly. The program was made to display to the console first, and then the GUI was attached on top.

Creatures

     has the state of every creature stored in a 2d array data structure. The creatures themselves all inherit from a base creature, so via polymorphism, the world is able to iterate through each creature and have them perform their own unique move, eat, breed, and die actions.

GUI

    The GUI of was created via Qt OpenGL. It features a start/stop button, reset button, and exit. It also has a checkbox for enabling infections. Finally, it lists a few stats for each creature: their population, and percentage of total.

Future

     is a completed course project. This was meant to be a learning experience and practice for C++, and a dip into GUI programming. T here are currently no plans to improve or renovate the project.
