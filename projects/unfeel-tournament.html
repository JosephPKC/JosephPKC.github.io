About

     is a 2D multiplayer fighting game mdae for a game development course at UCI (COMPSCI 113). Players control one of four emotions, and must utilize weapons scattered across the battlefield to defeat the others and be the last emotion standing.

The main menu.

The story.

Usage

Setting up a Game

    To start a game in, you must create a game, and decide on the game settings. You must decide how many lives each player gets (1 - 10), how many players (2 - 4), and how much time (5 min max). Each player will then have that many lives at the start, and the game will end when the time is reached.

Choosing the game settings.

    After the game settings are chosen, each player takes turns choosing an emotion. Each emotion can only be chosen once. Difference in emotions have no impact on gameplay, and are purely cosmetic.

Choosing characters.

    Once the characters are chosen, the level must be chosen. The game comes with 4 different levels: Mountain, Graveyard, Snow, Space. After that, the game begins.

Choosing a level.

Gameplay

    During the game, each player must move around, dodging each other's attacks, and picking up weapons and items. There are spawn points around the level, in which specific weapons can spawn in. Weapons and items have different spawn rates depending on the spawn point and the level. When a player picks up a weapon, he/she replaces their previously equipped weapon. If a player picks up an item, the item is consumed immediately and used.

In the Snow level, Happy fires the Bubble Shotgun at Worried. Meanwhile, Angry waits afar, patiently.

Worried, while enlarged by eating the Cake, fires the Mega Bubble Launcher in the Mountain level.

Happy fires the Bubble Sniper from afar in the Graveyard level.

Sad fires the Bubble Thrower at Angry in the Space level.

    When a player is hit with a weapon, they take damage. When they lose all of their health, they are killed and then respawned. This costs them a life. If a player loses all of their lives, they are out of the game. The last player standing wins, unless the game timer ends before that happens. If the game timer ends, the player with the most kills is determined to be the victor. There are also cases where nobody wins. This is when there is a tie.

Weapons and Items

    There are many different weapons and items:

  • Bubble Gun: A basic gun that fires bubbles.
  • Assault Bubble Gun: An automatic gun that has a high rate of fire.
  • Bubble Shotgun: A shotgun that fires 5 bubbles in a cone. It has a knockback that grows spaner with each bubble hit.
  • Bubble Sniper: A powerful gun that fires bubbles at very long ranges.
  • Mega Bubble Launcher: A launcher that fires a large, explosive bubble in a short arc.
  • Bubble Thrower: A weapon that fires a stream of tiny bubbles.
  • Baseball Bat: A regular ol' baseball bat with a span knockback.
  • Cake: An item that increases your maximum health, and makes you much bigger.
  • Star: An item that makes you move much faster and jump much higher.

Results

    Once the game is over, the players enter a results screen. Here, they can see each player's stats, including kills, deaths, damage dealt, and damage taken. Afterwards, the players are given the ending of the emotion that won, or the ending where no emotions win.

Controls

    At the main menu, you can see the controls for various players. To support 4 player local multiplayer, we provided XBox 360 controller support. Two players would use the keyboard + mouse, and the two others would use controllers.

Development

Overview

     was made in C# and Unity3D. The art, textures, sfx, and music were from opengameart.org and freesound.org

Characters

    The characters consist of a single controller script, the texture, and the animations. The controller allows players to move, jump, pick up weapons, use weapons, take damage, and die. It also controls when certain animations and sounds would go off, and also handles flipping of the textures when needed.

Weapons and Items

    The weapons and items each have their own scripts. They have a single controller script, and a texture. The controller allows the firing of the weapon, or consumption of the item, and plays sounds when needed. The weapon controllers also instantiate the bubbles when fired. The bubbles themselves have a controller of their own, which handles its movement and trajectory.

Levels

    The levels were created using assets from opengameart.org. Each level comprises of platforms, and the never-ending bottom that kills you if you fall. There are several spawn points for items and players all over the level.

Game Manager

    There is a single game manager that handles the logic of the game. The game manager interacts with the user interface to get values for the game, changes the scenes, loads levels, keeps track of the players and their lives, and determines the winner.

User Interface

    The user interface consists of 2 to 4 picture frames for each player, their health, and their lives. There is also a timer that counts down at the top center.

Documentation

    Along with the game, we had to create a design document for the course.

Future

     is a completed game. However, there are many improvements to be made. This includes a larger variety of weapons and items, more levels, dynamic obstacles in levels, and more customizable game settings. All in all, the core game is complete and functional; it just needs a bit more variety.
